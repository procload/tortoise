Learn with Toby the Galapagos Tortoise

The Galapogos Tortoise is the world's "rarest living creature" according to the Guinness Book of Records. This site was created to inform others about Galapagos Tortoises and share our love of this amazing species.

What does a Galapagos Tortoise Eat?

The Diet of a Galapagos Tortoise

Mostly green vegetation, such as grass, leaves, tree bark, berries and lichen

How much and how often do they eat?

They eat very slowly, but can consume up to 80 pounds of food a day. They can go up to one year without food or water!

How do they get food?

They forage for food in their surroundings.

Natural Surroundings

Volcanic lowlands and shrub land

Where do Galapagos Tortoises Live?

Natural Surroundings

They only live on the Galapagos Islands

Where can you find them?

They have lived on the Galapagos Islands for 3 million years!

Where does the animal make it home?

Offspring do not remain with their parents. Upon hatching, they take several weeks to dig out and then they are left to fend for themselves.

The Reproductive Cycle of the Galapagos Tortoise

Number of Offspring

Galapagos tortoises lay 1 to 4 clutches each season, with about 16 eggs each

Gestational Period

The gestation period is about 4 to 8 months

How long do the offspring remain with the parent?

Offspring do not remain with their parents. Upon hatching, they take several weeks to dig out and then they are left to fend for themselves.

Environment

Description

Galapagos tortoises are about 4 to 5 feet in length, and weigh up to 500 pounds! They breathe about four times per minute, and they cannot breathe underwater.

Lifespan

Can live to be over 100 years old!

Interesting Facts

They have lived on the Galapagos Islands for over 3 million years!
